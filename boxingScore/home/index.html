Scoring MMA and Boxing Fights

MMA

Scoring an MMA fight involves assessing each round independently using the 10-Point Must System. Judges evaluate striking, grappling, octagon control, and defense to determine the winner of each round. The fighter who dominates in these areas earns 10 points, while the opponent typically receives 9 points or less. Overall, judges prioritize effective techniques, damage inflicted, control, and aggression. The fighter with the highest total score wins the fight, with draws possible if rounds are evenly contested.

Boxing

Scoring a boxing match uses the 10-Point Must System, where judges award each round to the boxer who demonstrates superior punching, ring control, and defense. The winner of each round typically receives 10 points, while the opponent gets 9 points or less. Clean and impactful punching is prioritized, with effective ring generalship and defense also considered. The boxer with the highest total score wins the fight, with draws possible if rounds are evenly contested.
